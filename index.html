Bangladesh Delta Plan 2100

"Achieving safe,climate resilient and prosperous delta"

The Bangladesh Delta Plan (BDP) 2100 is a long term integrated techno-economic mega plan that integrates all delta-related sector plans and policies, enveloping a Delta Vision and strategies that make it possible to integrate sector plans and policies for the long term and to present actionable interventions with a roadmap for realization.
Government of Bangladesh has approved the Delta Plan 2100 on September 4, 2018 to secure the future of water resources and mitigate the likely effects of climate change and natural disasters.

Goals

The BDP 2100 proposes 6 BDP 2100 specific goals that contribute to achieving these higher-level goals

SAFETY
Ensure safety from floods and climate change related disasters.
WATER
Enhance water security and efficiency of water usages.
MANAGEMENT
Ensure sustainable and integrated river systems and estuaries management.
ECOSYSTEM
Conserve and preserve wetlands and ecosystems and promote their wise use.
GOVERNANCE
Develop effective institutions and equitable governance for in-country and trans-boundary water resources management.
OPTIMACY
Achieve optimal and integrated use of land and water resources.

Delta Plan Specific Goals

  1. Ensure Safety from Floods and Climate Change related Disasters

    This goal focuses on managing the risks of floods in the wet season and other climate change related disasters in the Delta including the coastal zone at a well-defined and acceptable level, reliable water system conditions for long term economic development and the performance of key societal and economic functions.The goal considers ensuring safety of lives and livelihood against flood, flash flood, drought, salinity intrusion, river bank erosion, cyclones, storm surge, etc. through introducing improved adaptation and mitigation options.

  2. Enhance Water Security and Efficiency of Water Usages

    This goal relates to water resources management especially during the dry season as well as water shortage during dry spells. Maintaining a balance between the economic developments, expanding population and the need to secure water for multifaceted uses is a core challenge of the BDP 2100. The goal aims to ensure reliable and adequate provision of freshwater to support equitable and sustainable economic development providing sanitation at acceptable levels in relation to defined standards as well as environmental, health, agricultural and industrial needs.

  3. Ensure Sustainable and Integrated River Systems and Estuaries Management

    This goal considers sustainable management of river systems and estuaries, considered as one of the cornerstones of the Bangladesh Delta. The river systems of the country offer key development ingredients for the economy, society and the environment.This goal seeks to foster sustainable river systems management by coping with its morphological dynamics and results in cost-effective, environment friendly and socially viable options for reclamation and developments of lands.

  4. Conserve and Preserve Wetlands and Ecosystems and Promote their Wise Use

    The goal uses the term ‘conservation’ as a more generic target to safeguard and maintain the wetlands, whereas ‘preservation’ is a more converging approach, to protect some sensitive ecosystem areas. Some of the components to be safeguarded under the goal are mangrove forests, water and riverine ecosystem, coastal greenbelt, seasonal and perennial wetlands, tidal freshwater frontier etc.

  5. Develop Effective Institutions and Equitable Governance for In-Country and Trans- Boundary Water Resources Management

    The goal aims to improve the existing water governance for tackling both the endogenous and exogenous challenges of water resources management. At the same time, the community level functioning of local institutions would also be rejuvenated. Key elements include: ensuring adequate financing for investment, operation and maintenance,private sector participation at appropriate levels, minimizing the gaps between planning and implementation,supporting integrated water resource management at regional and local levels, taking into account the interests of all prominent water users,knowledge development for integrated decision-making though modeling, data and information sharing and management .

  6. Achieve Optimal and Integrated Use of Land and Water Resources

    The primary quest of this goal is to ensure efficient integration and proper interaction between land and water use aspects. The goal would mainstream the inter-connected resources functions (drainage, navigation, etc.) as well. The key topics covered by the goal are land use, water use, and river transport network. Establishment of connectivity of water courses and inducing integration between different modes of resources interactions are emphasized under this goal.
